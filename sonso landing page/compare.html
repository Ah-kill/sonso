The Upgrade Trap

The feature you need is
always one plan away

Here's how it plays out on every major platform — real examples of features hidden behind paid gates.

🟠 Zoho CRM

Custom dashboards locked to Enterprise

You're on Professional, things are going well — but you need a custom dashboard to see your team's performance. That's an Enterprise feature. Upgrade or go without.

🟣 Odoo

Every module is a separate purchase

Need inventory + sales + HR? Each module is billed separately per user. Your ₹1,500/user bill quietly becomes ₹4,500/user as your needs grow.

🟢 Shopify

Advanced reports behind Advanced plan

Want to see which products are actually profitable? That's an Advanced plan feature. Basic stores get basic visibility — and pay ₹15,000/month more for the rest.

🟠 Zoho Books

Automation rules limited by plan

Need to auto-send invoices when a payment is received? You get 5 automations on Standard. Unlimited automations — the feature that saves your team hours — is on Professional only.

Final Verdict

Who each platform is really for

These platforms aren't bad — they're just built for a different problem than yours.

🟠 Zoho

Best for large, multi-department teams

If you have 100+ people and dedicated IT staff who can configure and maintain it — Zoho is powerful. For a 10–50 person business, it's overkill and slow to set up.

🟣 Odoo

Best for manufacturing & complex ERP

Odoo shines for factories and distributors with complex multi-step processes. For a service business or retail operation, the setup cost doesn't justify the outcome.

🟢 Shopify

Best for pure online retail

If you run a product store that needs a checkout, Shopify is excellent. For managing internal operations, bookings, field teams, or service workflows — it simply wasn't built for that.

⚡ So N So

Best for growing SMEs with specific operational needs

If you have a clear workflow you need to systemise — orders, bookings, inventory, leads, staff — and you want it done in a week without a year-long SaaS bill, this is built for you.
